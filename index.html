Bridging Instead of Replacing Online Coding Communities with AI through Community-Enriched Chatbot Designs

Junling Wang, Lahari Goswami, Gustavo Kreia Umbelino, Kiara Chau, Mrinmaya Sachan, April Wang
ETH Zurich
DOI Paper (PDF) Code

ChatCommunity grounds LLM responses in community notebooks and surfaces source previews with social signals to support trust and community engagement.

Abstract

LLM chatbots can help with coding tasks, but they often provide isolated responses without social learning context. Online coding communities (e.g., Kaggle) offer socially mediated learning through peer content and discussion, yet growing reliance on LLMs can reduce participation.

We introduce Community-Enriched AI, a design paradigm that embeds community knowledge and social signals into chatbot interactions. We instantiate this idea in ChatCommunity, a RAG-based chatbot that previews retrieved community posts with rich social metadata. Across two studies (N=28; N=12), we find the design increases perceived reliability, encourages engagement with community content, and supports learners in completing data science tasks.

How it works

ChatCommunity uses a RAG pipeline over Kaggle notebooks: it retrieves relevant notebook chunks, ranks them (relevance/votes/views), and generates an answer while showing source previews with community signals.
